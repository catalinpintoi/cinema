Fight Club (1999)

"Fight Club" is a 1999 American film directed by David Fincher and based on the 1996 novel of the same name by Chuck Palahniuk. The film stars Brad Pitt as Tyler Durden and Edward Norton as the unnamed narrator, two men who start an underground fight club as a way to escape their mundane lives and societal expectations.

The film is known for its dark humor, edgy social commentary, and iconic twists. It explores themes such as consumerism, masculinity, and the search for identity in modern society. The film's gritty visual style and raw energy helped establish David Fincher as a leading director in Hollywood.

Despite initially receiving mixed reviews upon its release, "Fight Club" has since gained a cult following and is considered one of the most influential films of the 1990s. The film's subversive themes and style have made it a favorite among audiences seeking to question societal norms and challenge the status quo. It is a must-watch for fans of thought-provoking cinema and is widely regarded as one of the most iconic films of its era.

Screen time: 2h 19m

Cast: Edward Norton, Brad Pitt, Meat Loaf, Zach Grenier, Richmond Arquette, David Andrews, George Maguire, Eugenie Bondurant, Christina Cabot
