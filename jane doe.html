The Autopsy of Jane Doe (2016)

"The Autopsy of Jane Doe" is a 2016 American horror film directed by André Øvredal and starring Emile Hirsch and Brian Cox. The film follows the story of a father and son coroner duo who receive a mysterious female body, Jane Doe, and are tasked with conducting an autopsy to determine the cause of death.

As the father and son perform the autopsy, they uncover increasingly strange and inexplicable findings about the body, leading to a terrifying and suspenseful series of events. The film is known for its slow-burning tension, intricate plot, and unexpected twists, as well as its strong performances by Hirsch and Cox.

"The Autopsy of Jane Doe" received critical acclaim upon its release, with many praising its inventive storytelling, effective scares, and haunting atmosphere. The film's success has led to a growing appreciation for director André Øvredal's unique approach to horror, which blends traditional genre elements with more nuanced themes and characters. It is a must-watch for fans of horror and suspense, and is widely regarded as one of the best horror films of the 2010s.
